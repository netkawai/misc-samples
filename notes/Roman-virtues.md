Roman virtues

Personal virtues
Auctoritas  
"Spritual Authority" The sense of one's social standing, built up through experience
Comitas 
"Humour" Ease of manner, courtesy, openness, and friendliness.
Clementia 
"Mercy" Mildness and gentleness
Dignitas 
"Dignity" the ability to behave in a calm controlled way even in a difficult situation.
Firmitas 
"Tenacity" Strength of mind, the ability to stick to one's purpose.
Frugalits 
"Frugalness" Economy and simplicity of style, without being misserly.
Gravitas 
"Gravity" A sense of the importantce of ther matter at hard, responsibility and eamestness.
Honestas 
"Respectibility" The image that onre presents as a respectable member of society.
Humanitas 
"Humanity" Refinement, civilization, learning, and being cultured.
Industria 
"Industriousness" Hard work
Prudentia 
"Prudence" Foresight, wisdom, and personal discretion.
Salubritas 
"Wholesomeness" Health and cleanliness
Serverritas 
"Stemness" Gravity, self-control
Veritas 
"Truthfulness" Honesty in dealing with others.
Pietas 
"Dutifulness" More than religious piety;a respect for the natural order society.
